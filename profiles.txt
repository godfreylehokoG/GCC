Muneiwa Sibiya
Muneiwa Sibiya is a Gospel Minister, Worship Leader, and Recording Artist. She was ordained as an Evangelist in 2019 at Church of God Mission Nigeria. Born in March 27th to Andrew Sibiya and Takalani Mutibi, Muneiwa was raised in Limpopo, South Africa, and grew up in Louis Trichardt. From a young age, Muneiwa showed a deep love for music, singing in primary school and participating in various singing competitions, including the National Eisteddfod Academy, school reviews, and Idols South Africa. Her passion for music developed into a calling. In 2018, Muneiwa was appointed Worship Team Leader at her church, where passion met purpose. Inspired by Myles Munroe's teachings, she believed that purpose is not merely discovered but walked into. Through worship and leadership, Muneiwa realized she was stepping into her divine assignment. In 2020, she recorded her debut single, 'Take Hold,' which placed in the Top 5 of the Heavenly Gates Gospel Awards nominations across Africa. Muneiwa hosted her first concert, 'Spontaneous Worship,' at Johannesburg Church of God Mission. In 2024, she held her first live recording and released the powerful worship single 'Yahweh,' earning nominations at the Women in Gospel Awards. Muneiwa is also academically accomplished, holding a Diploma in Civil Engineering, a Certificate in Project Management, IT certifications, and a Diploma in Applied Ministries.
